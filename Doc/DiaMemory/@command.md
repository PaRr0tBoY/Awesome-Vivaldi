### **How the Dia Memory System Works**

As you browse the internet using or chat with Dia, your activity is summarized on our servers to create “Memories” then sent back to you. Once created, we don’t store these summaries or your Memories on our end; they are saved on your device.

- For example, if you spend time researching restaurants in Oslo while using Dia, Dia might generate a Memory like: “User is planning a trip to Oslo and is interested in local dining.”

If a Memory is used as context to personalize Dia’s response to you, you will see a “Answered with Memory” tag at the bottom of the response. You can click it to see what Memories applied to that response. You do not need to use any special commands to apply Memories.

### **What exactly does Dia remember?**

Dia remembers contents of the pages you visited, conversations you’ve had with Dia, as well as summaries and analysis of this activity.

### **Where are my Memories stored? Is my private information safe?**

All Memory data is stored locally on your device. Summaries are generated securely by Dia and our AI processing partners do not keep copies on our servers. Your activity using Dia in incognito windows is never summarized or saved.

Our data protection system uses a****filter that runs wholly on your device. A set rule-based checks flag signals of sensitive content – for example, medical records, financial or payment details, login credentials, and similar data. If we classify the page as sensitive, we block it from the Memory system – so it will not be stored, transmitted, or used to generate Memories. Additionally, you can always exclude sites from Memory from the [Site Menu](https://public.diabrowser.com/what-is-dia-memory#block-240612503676804eb9c6fb364c83a28e).

### **Where and for how long are my content and Memories stored?**

- Contents of the pages you’ve visited and their summaries are stored for 7 days on your device. You can delete them by deleting the corresponding page from your browser’s history. Browsing history can be accessed in the Mac Menu Bar via _History &gt; Show all History._
- Your chats with Dia are stored on your device for as long as you remain a Dia user. You can access your past chats or delete all past chats from the History menu in the system Menu Bar.
- Memories generated from pages you visited and your chats with Dia are stored on your device for as long as you remain a Dia customer or until you delete them. You can delete memories by going into _Dia &gt; Settings &gt; Memory_ from the Mac Menu Bar.

### **How do I turn on Memory?**

- To turn on Memory: Visit _Dia &gt; Settings &gt; Memory_ in the Mac Menu Bar to turn on Memory.
- To turn on Memory for a specific site: Hover on the right side of your Address Bar. Then go to  _Site Menu &gt; Site Settings _and Check “Use in Memory”.

### **How do I turn off Memory?**

- For a specific site: Hover on the right side of your Address Bar. Then go to  _Site Menu__&gt; Site Settings _and Uncheck “Use in Memory”.
- To turn off Memory altogether: Visit _Dia &gt; Settings &gt; Memory_ in the Mac Menu Bar. When you do this, Dia will stop summarizing your activity and won’t use your past browsing activity or chats with Dia to personalize answers. Your existing Memories will be deleted.

### **How soon will my activity be used to personalize responses?**

It may take up to 8 hours for Memories to be generated about pages you visit or your chats with Dia.

### **How can I see what Dia has remembered about me?**

You can view Memories used to answer a question by clicking on “Answered with Memory.” We don’t currently support a way to view all Memories at once, we hope to support this soon.

### **How do I delete Memories?**

You can clear all of your Memories in _Dia &gt; Settings &gt; Memory_ in the Mac Menu Bar. Currently it’s not possible to delete individual Memories.

### **Can I ask Dia to remember specific things?**

Asking Dia to remember isn’t supported at the moment.

### **Can I turn Memory off for a specific question?**

You can click Answered with Memory &gt; ↻ (Retry without Memory) to get a response that’s not personalized with Memory.

### **How do I know if Memory was used in an answer?**

Look for the “Answered with Memory” note at the bottom of a response. Click it to see which Memories informed the answer.

### **What if I have feedback or want to suggest improvements?**

We’d love to hear from you! You can provide feedback directly by using the “Did Memory improve this answer?” bubble at the bottom of an answer. You can also [share feedback via support here](https://www.diabrowser.com/help). Your input helps us improve Memory for everyone.

### **Is Memory separate for each profile I use?**

Yes. Memory is specific to each profile and device. If you use Dia on multiple profiles or devices, Memories won’t be shared between them.
